# Internet and Email

## Content
The Internet is a global network that connects millions of computers worldwide. It allows people to share information, communicate, and access resources from anywhere. The World Wide Web (WWW) is the part of the Internet we browse with websites.

Email (Electronic Mail) lets us send messages instantly to anyone with an email address. An email address has two parts: username and domain name, separated by the @ symbol (e.g., student@example.com).

**Internet Safety Rules:**
1. Never share personal information (address, phone number, password)
2. Always ask parents before clicking links or downloading files
3. Use strong passwords (letters, numbers, and symbols)
4. Don't talk to strangers online
5. Tell a parent if something makes you uncomfortable

## Key Points
- Internet connects computers all over the world
- WWW (World Wide Web) is websites we browse
- Email = Electronic Mail for instant messaging
- Email addresses use the @ symbol (at sign)
- Always be safe and follow internet safety rules

## Vocabulary
- **Internet**: Global network of connected computers
- **Email**: Electronic mail - messages sent online
- **WWW**: World Wide Web - websites and web pages
- **Browser**: Software to view websites (Chrome, Firefox)
- **Password**: Secret code to protect your account
- **Domain name**: The part after @ in an email (e.g., gmail.com)
- **Username**: The part before @ in an email

## Fun Fact
The first email was sent in 1971 by Ray Tomlinson. He chose the @ symbol to separate the username from the computer name because it wasn't used much in names. He doesn't remember what the first email said - probably just "QWERTYUIOP"! 📧
