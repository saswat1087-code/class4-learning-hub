# Political Divisions of North and West India

## Content
India is divided into 28 states and 8 Union Territories. North and West India have many important states.

**North Indian States:**
1. **Jammu & Kashmir** - Summer capital: Srinagar, Winter capital: Jammu
2. **Himachal Pradesh** - Capital: Shimla
3. **Uttarakhand** - Capital: Dehradun
4. **Punjab** - Capital: Chandigarh
5. **Haryana** - Capital: Chandigarh
6. **Uttar Pradesh** - Capital: Lucknow
7. **Rajasthan** - Capital: Jaipur
8. **Delhi** (Union Territory) - Capital: New Delhi (National Capital)

**West Indian States:**
1. **Gujarat** - Capital: Gandhinagar
2. **Maharashtra** - Capital: Mumbai
3. **Goa** - Capital: Panaji

**Union Territories of North & West India:**
- **Delhi** - National Capital Territory
- **Chandigarh** - Shared capital of Punjab and Haryana
- **Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu**

**Major Cities:**
- Delhi: National capital (Red Fort, India Gate)
- Mumbai: Financial capital (Gateway of India)
- Jaipur: Pink City (Hawa Mahal)
- Amritsar: Golden Temple
- Ahmedabad: Sabarmati Ashram

## Key Points
- North India has 8 states
- West India has 3 states
- Delhi is the National Capital Territory
- Each state has a capital city
- States have their own government and Chief Minister

## Vocabulary
- **State**: A political division with its own government
- **Union Territory**: Area governed directly by Central Government
- **Capital**: City where the government is located
- **Chief Minister**: Head of state government
- **Governor**: Central government's representative in a state

## Fun Fact
Chandigarh is the capital of two states - Punjab and Haryana! It is a Union Territory that serves both. It was designed by the famous French architect Le Corbusier! 🏛️
