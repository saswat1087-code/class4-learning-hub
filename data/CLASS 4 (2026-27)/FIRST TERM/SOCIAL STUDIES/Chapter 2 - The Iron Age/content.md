# The Iron Age

## Content
The Iron Age was a period when people discovered how to use iron to make tools and weapons. It followed the Stone Age and Bronze Age.

**Timeline:**
- Stone Age: Used stone tools
- Bronze Age: Learned to mix copper and tin to make bronze
- Iron Age: Learned to make iron tools (stronger and cheaper than bronze)

**Why Iron was Important:**
- Iron is harder and stronger than bronze
- Iron ore (rock containing iron) was widely available
- Iron tools lasted longer and worked better
- Plows made farming easier (more food production)
- Better weapons for hunting and protection

**Life in the Iron Age:**
- People lived in villages with wooden houses
- They farmed using iron plows
- They made pottery and woven cloth
- They traded goods with other villages
- They had leaders and warriors

**Iron Age Inventions:**
- Iron plow (better farming)
- Iron sword and shield (better weapons)
- Iron axe (better for cutting wood)
- Iron nails (better building)
- Iron coins (trade and money)

## Key Points
- Iron Age came after Stone Age and Bronze Age
- Iron tools were stronger, cheaper, and lasted longer
- Iron plows helped grow more food
- Iron weapons helped protect villages
- People lived in villages and traded with others

## Vocabulary
- **Iron Age**: Period when people used iron tools
- **Iron Ore**: Rock containing iron
- **Smelting**: Process of extracting iron from ore
- **Plow**: Tool for tilling soil for farming
- **Forge**: Place where blacksmiths work with iron
- **Blacksmith**: Person who makes iron tools

## Fun Fact
The Iron Age began at different times around the world - in India around 1200 BCE, but in some parts of Africa as late as 200 BCE! 🔨
