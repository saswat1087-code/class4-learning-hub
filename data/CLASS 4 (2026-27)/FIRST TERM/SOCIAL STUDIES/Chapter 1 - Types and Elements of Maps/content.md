# Types and Elements of Maps

## Content
A map is a drawing of a place on a flat surface. Maps help us find locations and understand distances.

**Types of Maps:**
- **Political Map**: Shows boundaries of countries, states, cities
- **Physical Map**: Shows natural features (mountains, rivers, deserts)
- **Thematic Map**: Shows specific information (population, rainfall, crops)
- **Road Map**: Shows roads, highways, and transportation routes

**Elements of a Map:**
1. **Title**: Tells what the map shows
2. **Direction (Compass Rose)**: Shows North, South, East, West
3. **Scale**: Shows real distances on map
4. **Legend (Key)**: Explains symbols used on map
5. **Grid**: Helps locate places using coordinates

**Directions:**
- **Cardinal Directions**: North, South, East, West
- **Intermediate Directions**: Northeast, Southeast, Southwest, Northwest

**Using a Map:**
- Identify the title first
- Look at the compass rose for direction
- Use the legend to understand symbols
- Use the scale to measure distance

## Key Points
- Maps show places on a flat surface
- Political maps show boundaries, physical maps show nature
- Every map needs a title, direction, scale, and legend
- The compass rose shows directions (N, S, E, W)
- Different symbols represent different features

## Vocabulary
- **Map**: Drawing of a place on flat surface
- **Compass Rose**: Shows directions on a map
- **Scale**: Shows real distance on map
- **Legend**: Explains symbols on map
- **Political Map**: Shows boundaries
- **Physical Map**: Shows natural features

## Fun Fact
The oldest known world map is the Babylonian Map of the World, made around 600 BCE on a clay tablet! It shows Babylon surrounded by ocean! 🗺️
