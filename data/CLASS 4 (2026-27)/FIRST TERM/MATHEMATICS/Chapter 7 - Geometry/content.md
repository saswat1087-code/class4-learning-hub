# Geometry

## Content
Geometry is the study of shapes, sizes, and positions of figures.

**Basic Shapes:**
- **Point**: A exact position (•)
- **Line**: Straight path extending both directions
- **Line Segment**: Part of a line with two endpoints
- **Ray**: Part of a line with one endpoint
- **Angle**: Formed by two rays from a common point

**Types of Angles:**
- **Acute Angle**: Less than 90° (sharp)
- **Right Angle**: Exactly 90° (L-shape)
- **Obtuse Angle**: Between 90° and 180°
- **Straight Angle**: Exactly 180°

**Types of Triangles (by sides):**
- **Equilateral**: All three sides equal
- **Isosceles**: Two sides equal
- **Scalene**: No sides equal

**Types of Triangles (by angles):**
- **Acute-angled**: All angles less than 90°
- **Right-angled**: One angle = 90°
- **Obtuse-angled**: One angle greater than 90°

**Quadrilaterals (4-sided shapes):**
- **Square**: All sides equal, all angles 90°
- **Rectangle**: Opposite sides equal, all angles 90°
- **Rhombus**: All sides equal, opposite angles equal
- **Parallelogram**: Opposite sides parallel and equal
- **Trapezium**: One pair of parallel sides

**Circle Parts:**
- **Radius**: Distance from center to edge
- **Diameter**: Distance across circle (2 × radius)
- **Circumference**: Distance around circle
- **Chord**: Line joining two points on circle

## Key Points
- Point, line, line segment, ray, angle are basic elements
- Angles can be acute, right, obtuse, or straight
- Triangles are classified by sides and angles
- Quadrilaterals have 4 sides and 4 angles
- Circle has radius, diameter, and circumference

## Vocabulary
- **Geometry**: Study of shapes and sizes
- **Angle**: Space between two intersecting lines
- **Triangle**: 3-sided polygon
- **Quadrilateral**: 4-sided polygon
- **Radius**: Distance from center to edge of circle
- **Diameter**: Distance across circle (2 × radius)

## Fun Fact
The word "geometry" comes from Greek words "geo" (earth) and "metron" (measurement). Ancient Egyptians used geometry to re-measure land after floods! 📐
