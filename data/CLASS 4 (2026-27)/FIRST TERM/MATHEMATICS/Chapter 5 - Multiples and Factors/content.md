# Multiples and Factors

## Content
**Multiples:**
A multiple is the product of a number multiplied by any whole number.

Example - Multiples of 3:
3 × 1 = 3
3 × 2 = 6
3 × 3 = 9
3 × 4 = 12
3 × 5 = 15
So multiples of 3 are: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24...

**Properties of Multiples:**
- Every number is a multiple of itself
- Every number is a multiple of 1
- There are infinite multiples of a number

**Factors:**
Factors are numbers that divide exactly into another number.

Example - Factors of 12:
1 × 12 = 12
2 × 6 = 12
3 × 4 = 12
So factors of 12 are: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

**Properties of Factors:**
- 1 is a factor of every number
- Every number is a factor of itself
- The smallest factor is 1, largest is the number itself

**Difference between Multiples and Factors:**
- Multiples are larger numbers (like 12, 24, 36...)
- Factors are smaller numbers (like 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12)

## Key Points
- Multiples are what you get when you multiply
- Factors are numbers that divide exactly
- Every number has at least two factors (1 and itself)
- There are infinite multiples but finite factors
- Multiples are greater than or equal to the number

## Vocabulary
- **Multiple**: Product of a number and a whole number
- **Factor**: A number that divides exactly into another number
- **Product**: Result of multiplication
- **Divisible**: Can be divided evenly
- **Prime Number**: Has exactly 2 factors (1 and itself)
- **Composite Number**: Has more than 2 factors

## Fun Fact
The number 1 is special - it has only one factor (itself). It's neither prime nor composite! 🔢
