# HCF and LCM

## Content
**HCF (Highest Common Factor):**
The largest number that divides two or more numbers exactly.

Example: Find HCF of 12 and 18

Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
Common factors: 1, 2, 3, 6
HCF = 6 (largest common factor)

**LCM (Lowest Common Multiple):**
The smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.

Example: Find LCM of 4 and 6

Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24...
Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30...
Common multiples: 12, 24...
LCM = 12 (smallest common multiple)

**Prime Factorization Method:**
- Break numbers into prime factors
- HCF = product of common prime factors (lowest power)
- LCM = product of all prime factors (highest power)

Example: 12 = 2² × 3, 18 = 2 × 3²
HCF = 2¹ × 3¹ = 6
LCM = 2² × 3² = 36

## Key Points
- HCF is the largest common factor
- LCM is the smallest common multiple
- HCF ≤ each number, LCM ≥ each number
- Product of two numbers = HCF × LCM
- Use prime factorization for large numbers

## Vocabulary
- **HCF**: Highest Common Factor
- **LCM**: Lowest Common Multiple
- **Prime Factorization**: Breaking into prime numbers
- **Common Factor**: Factor shared by both numbers
- **Common Multiple**: Multiple shared by both numbers

## Fun Fact
HCF is also called GCD (Greatest Common Divisor). The Euclidean algorithm for finding GCD is over 2000 years old! 📜
