# The Big Race

## Content
The Big Race is a story about determination, teamwork, and believing in yourself. A small, slow animal (often a tortoise or similar) enters a race against much faster animals. Everyone laughs at the idea. But through persistence, clever thinking, and never giving up, the underdog wins the race.

The story teaches that speed and strength are not the only keys to success. Patience, determination, and strategy can overcome natural disadvantages.

**The Moral:** "Slow and steady wins the race." It's not always the fastest who succeeds, but the one who keeps going and never gives up.

## Key Points
- Don't underestimate anyone
- Persistence and determination are valuable
- Believing in yourself is important
- Hard work can overcome natural disadvantages
- Never give up, even when others doubt you

## Vocabulary
- **Determination**: Firmness of purpose; not giving up
- **Persistence**: Continuing steadily despite difficulties
- **Underdog**: Person expected to lose
- **Strategy**: A plan to achieve a goal
- **Overcome**: Succeed in dealing with a problem

## Fun Fact
Aesop's fable "The Tortoise and the Hare" has been told for over 2,500 years and has been translated into hundreds of languages! 🐢🐇
