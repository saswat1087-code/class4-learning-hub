# The Grateful Snow Crane

## Content
This is a beautiful Japanese folk tale about kindness and gratitude. An old man found a wounded snow crane caught in a trap. He freed the crane and cared for it until it healed. The crane flew away, leaving the old man happy that he could help.

That night, a beautiful young woman appeared at the old man's door. She said she had no family and asked to stay. The kind old man welcomed her. The woman started weaving the most beautiful silk cloth. She sold it for a high price, and the old man became rich.

But the woman had one request: "Please never watch me while I weave." One day, curiosity got the better of the old man. He peeked into the weaving room. To his surprise, he saw the snow crane plucking its own feathers to weave the silk cloth.

The crane said, "Now that you have seen my true form, I must leave." It transformed back into a crane and flew away, but it continued to visit the old man as a token of gratitude.

The story teaches that we should keep our promises and respect others' privacy. True kindness is rewarded, but broken trust has consequences.

## Key Points
- Kindness to animals is rewarded
- We should keep our promises
- Respect others' privacy and requests
- Gratitude is a powerful virtue
- Greed and curiosity can lead to loss

## Vocabulary
- **Grateful**: Feeling thankful
- **Crane**: A tall, long-necked bird
- **Folk Tale**: Traditional story passed down through generations
- **Gratitude**: Thankfulness
- **Curiosity**: Desire to know or see something
- **Transformed**: Changed form or appearance

## Fun Fact
In Japanese culture, the crane is a symbol of longevity, good fortune, and loyalty. It is believed that folding 1,000 paper cranes grants a wish! 🕊️
