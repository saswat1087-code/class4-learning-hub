# Adjectives - Kinds and Degrees

## Content
Adjectives are describing words. They tell us more about nouns (people, places, animals, or things).

**Kinds of Adjectives:**

1. **Adjective of Quality** - Tells what kind
   - Examples: big, small, red, beautiful, tall, sweet
   - Sentence: She wore a **red** dress.

2. **Adjective of Quantity** - Tells how much
   - Examples: some, any, much, little, enough, all
   - Sentence: There is **some** water in the glass.

3. **Adjective of Number** - Tells how many
   - Examples: one, two, first, second, many, several
   - Sentence: **Three** boys are playing.

4. **Demonstrative Adjective** - Points to specific noun
   - Examples: this, that, these, those
   - Sentence: **This** book is mine.

5. **Possessive Adjective** - Shows ownership
   - Examples: my, your, his, her, its, our, their
   - Sentence: **My** bicycle is new.

**Degrees of Comparison:**

1. **Positive Degree** - Describes one thing
   - Example: Ram is **tall**.

2. **Comparative Degree** - Compares two things
   - Example: Shyam is **taller** than Ram.

3. **Superlative Degree** - Compares three or more things
   - Example: Mohan is the **tallest** of all.

**Rules for Comparison:**
- Short words (1 syllable): add -er, -est (tall → taller → tallest)
- Words ending in y: change y to i, add -er, -est (happy → happier → happiest)
- Long words (2+ syllables): use more, most (beautiful → more beautiful → most beautiful)
- Irregular: good → better → best, bad → worse → worst

## Key Points
- Adjectives describe nouns
- There are 5 kinds of adjectives
- Degrees of comparison: positive, comparative, superlative
- Use -er/-est for short words, more/most for long words
- Some adjectives have irregular comparisons

## Vocabulary
- **Adjective**: Describing word for a noun
- **Positive Degree**: Basic form of adjective
- **Comparative Degree**: Compares two things
- **Superlative Degree**: Compares three or more things
- **Demonstrative**: Points to specific things (this, that)
- **Possessive**: Shows ownership (my, your)

## Fun Fact
The word "adjective" comes from the Latin "adjectivum" meaning "added to." Adjectives are added to nouns to give more information! 📝
