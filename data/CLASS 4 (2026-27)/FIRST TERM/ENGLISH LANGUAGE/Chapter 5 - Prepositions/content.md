# Prepositions

## Content
A preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word in the sentence. Prepositions often tell us where, when, or how something happens.

**Common Prepositions:**
in, on, at, under, above, below, behind, in front of, between, among, beside, near, far, through, across, over, into, out of, up, down, with, without, for, from, to, by, of, about

**Prepositions of Place (Where):**
- **in**: inside (The book is **in** the bag)
- **on**: on top of (The pen is **on** the table)
- **under**: below (The cat is **under** the chair)
- **behind**: at the back (He is **behind** the door)
- **in front of**: before (She is sitting **in front of** me)
- **between**: in the middle of two (The park is **between** the school and the hospital)
- **beside**: next to (The lamp is **beside** the bed)
- **above**: higher than (The fan is **above** the table)
- **below**: lower than (The basement is **below** the ground floor)

**Prepositions of Time (When):**
- **at**: specific time (The class starts **at** 9 o'clock)
- **in**: months, years, parts of day (I was born **in** May; She will come **in** the morning)
- **on**: days, dates (We will meet **on** Monday; **On** 15th August)

**Prepositions of Direction (Where to):**
- **to**: towards (She is going **to** school)
- **into**: inside (He jumped **into** the pool)
- **towards**: in the direction of (He ran **towards** the house)
- **through**: from one side to another (The train went **through** the tunnel)

**Prepositions of Manner (How):**
- **by**: method (She travels **by** bus)
- **with**: using something (He cut the paper **with** scissors)

## Key Points
- Prepositions show relationship between words
- Prepositions often tell position, time, or direction
- Common prepositions: in, on, at, under, behind, between
- A preposition is usually followed by a noun or pronoun
- The noun/pronoun after a preposition is called the object

## Vocabulary
- **Preposition**: Word showing relationship
- **Object of Preposition**: Noun/pronoun after a preposition
- **Place**: Where something is located
- **Time**: When something happens
- **Direction**: Where something is going
- **Manner**: How something is done

## Fun Fact
The word "preposition" comes from Latin "praeponere" meaning "to put before." Prepositions usually come before the noun they relate to! 📍
