# The Sentence

## Content
A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. Every sentence begins with a capital letter and ends with a punctuation mark.

**Types of Sentences:**

1. **Declarative (Statement)** - Makes a statement
   - Example: The sky is blue.
   - Ends with a period (.)

2. **Interrogative (Question)** - Asks a question
   - Example: What is your name?
   - Ends with a question mark (?)

3. **Exclamatory (Exclamation)** - Shows strong feeling
   - Example: What a beautiful day!
   - Ends with an exclamation mark (!)

4. **Imperative (Command)** - Gives a command or request
   - Example: Please close the door.
   - Ends with a period or exclamation mark

**Parts of a Sentence:**
- **Subject**: Who or what the sentence is about
- **Predicate**: What the subject does or is

Example: "The dog runs fast"
- Subject: The dog
- Predicate: runs fast

## Key Points
- A sentence expresses a complete thought
- Every sentence starts with a capital letter
- Every sentence ends with a punctuation mark (. ? !)
- There are 4 types of sentences
- Every sentence has a subject and a predicate

## Vocabulary
- **Sentence**: A group of words with a complete thought
- **Subject**: Who or what the sentence is about
- **Predicate**: What the subject does or is
- **Declarative**: A statement sentence
- **Interrogative**: A question sentence
- **Exclamatory**: An excited or emotional sentence
- **Imperative**: A command sentence

## Fun Fact
The shortest complete sentence in English is "I am" (2 words). Some say "Go!" is also a complete sentence because the subject "you" is implied! 🎯
