# Adaptation in Plants

## Content
Adaptation means special features that help plants survive in their environment.

**Plants in Deserts (Cactus):**
- Thick, fleshy stems store water
- Spines instead of leaves (reduce water loss)
- Deep roots to reach underground water
- Can survive with very little water

**Plants in Water (Aquatic Plants):**
- Lotus: Hollow stems float on water
- Hydrilla: Soft stems bend with water flow
- Air spaces in stems help them float
- Some have roots in soil, leaves floating

**Plants in Mountains:**
- Pine and fir trees: Cone-shaped to shed snow
- Needle-like leaves reduce water loss
- Sloping branches prevent snow buildup
- Thick bark protects from cold

**Plants in Plains:**
- Deciduous trees (shed leaves in winter)
- Broad leaves to capture more sunlight
- Deep roots for stability

**Mangroves (Coastal Areas):**
- Breathing roots (pneumatophores) stick out of soil
- Can survive in salty water
- Roots provide support in muddy soil

**Carnivorous Plants (Insect-eating):**
- Grow in soil without enough nutrients
- Trap and digest insects (Venus flytrap, Pitcher plant)
- Get nutrients from insects instead of soil

## Key Points
- Adaptations help plants survive in different environments
- Desert plants store water and reduce water loss
- Aquatic plants have air spaces to float
- Mountain plants have cone shapes to shed snow
- Mangroves have breathing roots for oxygen

## Vocabulary
- **Adaptation**: Special feature to survive in environment
- **Desert**: Very dry place with little water
- **Aquatic**: Living in water
- **Deciduous**: Trees that shed leaves in winter
- **Pneumatophores**: Breathing roots of mangroves
- **Carnivorous**: Insect-eating plants

## Fun Fact
Some plants can survive forest fires! The Banksia plant has seeds that only open and grow after a fire. Fire actually helps them reproduce! 🔥
