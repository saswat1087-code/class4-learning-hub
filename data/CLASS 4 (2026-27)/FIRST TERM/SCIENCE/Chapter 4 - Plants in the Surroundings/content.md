# Plants in the Surroundings and Environment

## Content
Plants are living things that make their own food. They are all around us and are essential for life on Earth.

**Parts of a Plant:**
- **Roots**: Absorb water and minerals from soil
- **Stem**: Carries water and food to all parts
- **Leaves**: Make food through photosynthesis
- **Flowers**: Help in reproduction (make seeds)
- **Fruits**: Protect seeds and help spread them

**Photosynthesis:**
Plants make their own food using:
- Sunlight (energy)
- Carbon dioxide (from air)
- Water (from soil)
- Chlorophyll (green pigment in leaves)

**Types of Plants:**
- **Trees**: Tall, woody stems (mango, banyan)
- **Shrubs**: Medium-sized, bushy (rose, hibiscus)
- **Herbs**: Small, soft stems (tomato, grass)
- **Climbers**: Need support to grow (grapevine, money plant)
- **Creepers**: Grow along the ground (watermelon, pumpkin)

**Importance of Plants:**
- Provide oxygen for breathing
- Provide food (fruits, vegetables, grains)
- Provide wood for furniture and paper
- Provide medicine
- Provide shade and cool the air
- Prevent soil erosion

## Key Points
- Plants make their own food through photosynthesis
- Plants need sunlight, water, air, and soil to grow
- Different types of plants: trees, shrubs, herbs, climbers, creepers
- Plants give us oxygen, food, wood, and medicine
- We must protect plants and trees

## Vocabulary
- **Photosynthesis**: Process by which plants make food
- **Chlorophyll**: Green pigment in leaves
- **Oxygen**: Gas we breathe that plants produce
- **Carbon Dioxide**: Gas plants use that we breathe out
- **Erosion**: Wearing away of soil by wind or water

## Fun Fact
A large tree can produce enough oxygen for four people to breathe every day! The Amazon rainforest produces 20% of the world's oxygen! 🌳
