# Formatting in MS Word 2013

## Content
Formatting means making text look attractive and organized. MS Word 2013 offers many formatting options.

**Font Formatting (Home Tab → Font Group):**

- **Font Style**: Change the typeface (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
- **Font Size**: Make text larger or smaller
- **Bold (Ctrl+B)**: Makes text darker and thicker
- **Italic (Ctrl+I)**: Slants text to the right
- **Underline (Ctrl+U)**: Adds a line under text
- **Font Color**: Change text color
- **Highlight**: Add background color to text

**Paragraph Formatting (Home Tab → Paragraph Group):**

- **Alignment**: Left, Center, Right, Justify
- **Line Spacing**: Space between lines (1.0, 1.5, 2.0)
- **Bullets and Numbering**: Create lists
- **Indentation**: Move text away from margin

**Page Formatting:**

- **Page Orientation**: Portrait (vertical) or Landscape (horizontal)
- **Page Size**: A4, Letter, etc.
- **Margins**: Space around the page edges

## Key Points
- Use Bold, Italic, and Underline to emphasize important text
- Alignment options: Left, Center, Right, Justify
- Use bullets for unordered lists, numbering for ordered lists
- Ctrl+B = Bold, Ctrl+I = Italic, Ctrl+U = Underline
- Change font size to make headings larger

## Vocabulary
- **Font**: The style of text characters
- **Alignment**: How text is positioned on the line
- **Bold**: Makes text darker and thicker
- **Italic**: Slants text to the right
- **Underline**: Adds a line under text
- **Justify**: Aligns text to both left and right margins

## Fun Fact
The most used font in the world is Arial. Times New Roman was specifically designed for newspapers to save space! ✏️
