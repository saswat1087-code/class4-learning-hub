# AI and Humans

## Content
AI stands for Artificial Intelligence. It is the ability of computers to think and learn like humans. AI is changing how we live and work.

**What AI Can Do:**
- Recognize faces in photos
- Understand spoken commands (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant)
- Play games like chess
- Drive cars automatically
- Recommend movies and songs
- Help doctors diagnose diseases

**How AI Learns:**
- AI systems are trained using lots of examples
- They find patterns in data
- They improve over time with more practice

**Humans vs AI:**
- **Humans are better at**: Being creative, understanding emotions, making moral decisions
- **AI is better at**: Processing large amounts of data quickly, not getting tired, perfect memory

**AI in Daily Life:**
- Smartphones (face unlock, voice assistants)
- Social media (content recommendations)
- Online shopping (product suggestions)
- Email (spam filtering)
- Navigation apps (traffic predictions)

## Key Points
- AI means computers that can think and learn
- AI needs data to learn and improve
- AI can recognize faces, understand speech, and make predictions
- AI helps humans but cannot replace human creativity
- We use AI every day without realizing it

## Vocabulary
- **AI**: Artificial Intelligence - machines that can think and learn
- **Machine Learning**: Teaching computers using data
- **Algorithm**: Step-by-step instructions for solving problems
- **Data**: Information used to train AI
- **Automation**: Using machines to do tasks automatically

## Fun Fact
The term "Artificial Intelligence" was first used in 1956 at a conference at Dartmouth College. Today's AI would seem like magic to people back then! 🤖
